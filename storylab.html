Hook
Grab your reader from the very first word
0 words
▼
✨ Writing tips for your hook
  • Start with a bold, surprising statement that makes the reader think "What?!"
  • Or drop us straight into action — something is already happening
  • Or ask a question the reader desperately wants answered
  • 1–3 sentences only. Make every word earn its place.

Introduction
Who, what, where, when — set the scene
0 words
▼
✨ Writing tips for your introduction
  • Introduce your main character — who are they and what do we need to know?
  • Paint the setting — use your senses (sight, sound, smell, touch, feeling)
  • Set up the normal world before things go wrong
  • Hint at the problem without revealing it — create a sense of unease or wonder
  • Give the reader a reason to care about your character
🍅
Rising Action
The problem builds — tension and trouble
0 words
▼
✨ Writing tips for rising action
  • Introduce the main problem or conflict — what goes wrong?
  • Show how your character reacts — thoughts, feelings, actions
  • Add obstacles — things that make solving the problem harder
  • Build suspense — slow down time during tense moments
  • Use your character's fears or weaknesses against them
  • Use short sentences to speed up the pace when needed
🍖
Climax
The biggest, most powerful moment
0 words
▼
✨ Writing tips for your climax
  • This is the turning point — the moment everything changes
  • Slow WAY down — write every second in detail
  • Use all five senses — make the reader feel like they're there
  • Show your character making a crucial decision or taking action
  • Use short, punchy sentences for maximum impact
  • This should be your most vivid, emotional writing
🧀
Falling Action
After the peak — things begin to settle
0 words
▼
✨ Writing tips for falling action
  • Show the immediate aftermath of the climax
  • How has your character changed? What are they feeling?
  • Tie up loose ends — what happened to other characters?
  • Slow the pace back down — use longer, calmer sentences
  • Let the reader breathe before the ending
🍞
Resolution
How it ends — what your character learns
0 words
▼
✨ Writing tips for your resolution
  • How has your character grown or changed from the start?
  • End with something that feels earned — not rushed
  • Consider echoing something from your hook (a full-circle ending)
  • Leave the reader with a feeling — hope, wonder, satisfaction, or even a twist
  • A great last line is worth spending time on
